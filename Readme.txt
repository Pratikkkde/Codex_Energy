This project analyzes data related to various soft drink consumption and preferences among various demographics. The analysis aims to provide insights into consumer behavior, preferences, and the effectiveness of marketing strategies in the energy drink market. The findings will help guide product development and marketing efforts for energy drinks.

The analysis addresses key questions like:

Who prefers energy drinks more? (male/female/non-binary?)
Which age group prefers energy drinks more?
Which type of marketing reaches the most youth (15-30)?
What are the preferred ingredients of energy drinks among respondents?
What packaging preferences do respondents have for energy drinks?
Who are the current market leaders?
What are the primary reasons consumers prefer those brands over ours?
Which marketing channel can be used to reach more customers?
How effective are different marketing strategies and channels in reaching our customers?
What do people think about our brand? (overall rating)
Which cities do we need to focus more on?
Where do respondents prefer to purchase energy drinks?
What are the typical consumption situations for energy drinks among respondents?
What factors influence respondents' purchase decisions, such as price range and limited edition packaging?
Which area of business should we focus more on for our product development? (Branding/taste/availability)

The SQL queries used to answer the questions above are included in the Codex_energy_analysis.sql file. Each query is designed to extract relevant information from the dataset, allowing for a comprehensive analysis of consumer preferences and market trends.

The results of the analysis provide valuable insights into consumer behavior, including demographic preferences, effective marketing channels, and key areas for product development. The findings can be instrumental in shaping future marketing strategies and product offerings for a dummy energy drink brand Codex.
